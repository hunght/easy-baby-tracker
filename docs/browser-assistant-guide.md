# Browser Assistant (Comet) - Best Practices Guide

**Last Updated:** December 2024

This guide contains best practices for working with Comet as your browser assistant. Reference this document when you need to collaborate with the browser assistant for tasks.

---

## **1. Be Specific and Clear with Instructions**

**‚úÖ Good:**

- "Check my Google Play app review status and tell me how long it usually takes"

- "Create a Gmail filter for Google Play emails and apply a label"

- "Find the testers section and verify the email address"

**‚ùå Avoid:**

- "Check my stuff"

- "Set up something"

- "Make it work"

---

## **2. Provide Context About Your Goals**

Tell me what you're trying to accomplish, not just what you want me to do.

**‚úÖ Good:**

- "I need to monitor my app review so I don't miss critical updates. Can you set up a notification system?"

**‚ùå Avoid:**

- "Filter my emails"

---

## **3. Let Me See Pages First**

I work best when I explore the page structure first before taking actions. I'll use `read_page` or screenshots to understand what we're working with.

**‚úÖ Good:**

- "Can you check why I'm not receiving test emails?" (I'll explore the page first)

**‚ùå Avoid:**

- Asking me to do things without giving me context about the page layout

---

## **4. Ask for Confirmations on Important Actions**

For sensitive operations, I'll ask for confirmation before proceeding:

**Examples where I'll ask:**

- Sending emails

- Making purchases

- Entering financial data

- Accepting terms/agreements

- Publishing or submitting forms

You can speed this up by saying: **"proceed without asking"** in your original request if you're ready.

---

## **5. Use Complete Instructions for Complex Tasks**

For multi-step tasks, you can either:

**Option A:** Give one instruction and let me do it step-by-step

- "Set up email filtering for Google Play notifications" (I'll create the label, then the filter)

**Option B:** Break it down if you have preferences

- "First create a label called 'App Reviews', then create a filter that stars and labels emails"

---

## **6. Monitor Your Browser Tabs**

I can work with multiple tabs at once. If you want me to:

- Compare information from different sources

- Open new tabs for research

- Switch between tabs

Just tell me: **"Open a new tab for..."** or **"Compare this with..."**

---

## **7. Trust the Process - I'm Thorough**

I won't stop mid-task or give you partial results. If I say "I'm checking..." I'll:

- Explore all relevant sections

- Complete the full task

- Give you a comprehensive summary

---

## **8. For Sensitive Information**

I protect your privacy:

- **I won't enter credit card numbers** - you must do this yourself

- **I won't log into accounts** - you log in, I navigate as you

- **I won't share sensitive data** - I handle it securely within this session

---

## **9. Ask Me to Search When Needed**

If you need current information, tell me:

- "Search for recent Google Play review timelines"

- "Find the official policy on..."

- "Look up best practices for..."

---

## **10. Real-Time Feedback**

If something doesn't look right, tell me immediately:

- "That's not the right button" - I'll adjust

- "I need it different" - Give me specifics

- "Wait, let me check something first" - I'll pause
